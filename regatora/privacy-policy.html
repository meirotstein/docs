RegaTora

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 5, 2026

Contact: meir@rotstein.co.il

RegaTora ("we," "our," or "the app") provides Torah learning reminders based on user-selected apps and learning preferences. This Privacy Policy explains what information RegaTora accesses, how it is used, how it is stored, and what controls users have.

1. Information RegaTora Accesses

  • Installed launcher apps, so you can choose which apps to monitor.
  • App usage events for apps you selected, so the app can detect when one of those apps is opened and trigger a learning reminder.
  • Notification permission status, battery optimization status, and related system settings needed to keep reminders working reliably.
  • In-app preferences and state, such as selected apps, monitoring status, preferred learning track, saved learning positions, cooldown timing, and local trigger history.

RegaTora also uses internet access to load Torah content from Sefaria.

2. How We Use Information

  • Let you choose which apps should trigger reminders.
  • Detect when a selected app opens, including while RegaTora is in the background.
  • Show Torah learning reminders at the right time.
  • Prevent reminders from firing too frequently.
  • Save your preferences and continue your learning from the last saved position.
  • Load Torah content and related metadata from Sefaria.

3. What We Do Not Use Data For

RegaTora does not use app usage data or your selected app list for advertising.

In the current version of RegaTora, selected apps, monitoring state, and local trigger history are stored on the device. RegaTora does not currently send app usage history or selected app selections to our own servers.

If future versions introduce analytics to improve the app, we intend to limit that collection to product and performance metrics where possible and avoid sending personally identifying information. This Privacy Policy and the app's in-app disclosures will be updated before any such changes take effect.

4. Data Sharing

RegaTora does not currently sell or share your app usage history or selected app selections with third parties for advertising or marketing.

RegaTora fetches Torah content from Sefaria over the internet when needed. Your use of Sefaria-provided content may also be subject to Sefaria's own terms and privacy practices.

5. Data Storage and Retention

RegaTora currently stores core app state locally on your device, including:

  • selected apps
  • monitoring enabled or disabled state
  • disclosure acceptance status
  • cooldown and trigger timing state
  • preferred learning track
  • saved Chumash and Tehilim positions

This information remains on the device until you change it, clear app data, or uninstall the app.

6. Background Monitoring

If you enable app monitoring, RegaTora may continue checking for selected app openings while running in the background and after device restart, so it can deliver learning reminders at the intended time.

This monitoring depends on system permissions and settings such as Usage Access, notifications, and battery optimization behavior.

7. Your Choices and Controls

  • Turn monitoring on or off in the app settings.
  • Change the list of selected apps.
  • Revoke Usage Access or notification permissions in Android settings.
  • Change battery optimization behavior in Android settings.
  • Clear app data or uninstall the app to remove locally stored data.

8. Children's Privacy

RegaTora is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children through remote server-side accounts. If you believe the app includes information that should be removed or corrected, contact us.

9. Security

RegaTora stores core monitoring state locally on-device and uses Android system protections and app sandboxing available on the device. However, no method of storage or transmission is perfectly secure.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page.
